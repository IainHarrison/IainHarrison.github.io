About

I am a creative and enthusiastic games developer. I enjoy the creation process of games development from conception to realisation. As a games developer I am experienced working in teams, collaborating with artists and designers to create a game. I’m not afraid to give suggestions, and also recognise my own strengths and those of my teammates. For fun I often create games by myself which includes doing the art, sound and design and of course the part I find most enjoyable, programming. If you were to hire me, you would be getting a confident and passionate game developer that will be sure to pull his weight. I am currently in my second year at Saxion University in Enschede, Netherlands; the course has taught me many things including C#, C++, Unity, and the creation of game engines. I am now looking for an internship to continue my professional development and apply the skills I have learned to the workplace.

The Wispering Guardian

“The Wispering Guardian” is a game that is based on campaign to spread awareness about littering in the forest. You play as the wisp with the ability to fly in a large open forest environment in which you must pick up the trash in the forest to activate all forest shrines to help his friends. While completing this goal, the wisp must avoid being spotted by enemies throughout the whole game or else his energy meter/health drops. This project was created by a group of 6 students (3 Artists, 2 Designers, 1 Engineer) within a 3-week span. As the only engineer in the group, I was responsible for all the coding of the game (Unity, C#). I created many in-game mechanics like dashing, possessing objects, spooking enemies as well as an energy meter that acts as his health.

Procedurally generated city

Visually inspired by “Mafia 3”, a group of 6 students (3 Artists, 2 Designers, 1 Engineer) created a completely self-made procedural city in Unity (C#). The outcome is a Tool that can be used by Artists and Designers in projects in the Editor without touching any code. As the engineer of the group, I was responsible for all the coding. With the finished project, all the assets are modular, building-sizes are editable manually in editor-time, the size of the city and the number of roads in each direction can be specified, all roofs and windows are configurable in the editor and it has a day/night- cycle. To create all of this, only 12 3D models (road tiles, walls, window, roofs) were used.

LeARnle

To combat the teacher shortage in the Netherlands, a group of 6 students (3 Artists, 2 Designers, 1 Engineer) created “LeArnle” - an app and a book that works with Augmented Reality to teach children, in elementary school, certain subjects. The app contains the questions and stories that the students goes through and the book contains the images that can be scanned by the app. As the engineer of the group, I researched a lot on different hardware and technologies that can be used in schools. As we ended up with AR, I coded the whole application (Unity, C#) for the AR learning experience.
